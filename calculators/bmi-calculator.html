Understanding Body Mass Index

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used measurement that estimates whether a person has a healthy body weight relative to their height. Healthcare professionals use it as a screening tool to identify weight categories that may lead to health risks such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, or metabolic disorders.

FAQs

What is considered a healthy BMI?

A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy for most adults.

Is BMI always accurate?

BMI is useful for population-level screening but may not accurately reflect body composition for athletes or individuals with high muscle mass.

Why do doctors still use BMI?

Because it is simple, fast, and useful for identifying general weight categories across large populations.
