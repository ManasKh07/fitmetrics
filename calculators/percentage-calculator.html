Understanding Percentages

A percentage expresses a number as a fraction of 100. The word comes from the Latin per centum, meaning "by the hundred." Percentages are used in finance (interest rates, tax), health (body fat, test scores), statistics, and everyday calculations like discounts and tips.

FAQs

How do you calculate a percentage manually?

Divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100. For example: 25 ÷ 200 × 100 = 12.5%.

Can a percentage exceed 100?

Yes. A value over 100% means the amount exceeds the original total. For example, if sales grew from 100 to 250, that is a 150% increase.

What is the difference between percentage and percentage points?

Percentage points measure the arithmetic difference between two percentages. If a rate rises from 5% to 8%, it increased by 3 percentage points but by 60% in relative terms.
