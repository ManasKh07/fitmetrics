Understanding Percentages

A percentage represents a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. The term originates from the Latin phrase per centum, meaning "by the hundred."

Percentages are widely used in finance, statistics, academics and everyday calculations — from exam scores and discounts to tax rates and financial growth.

If you are calculating health metrics you might also want to estimate your Body Mass Index or determine your daily calorie expenditure.

FAQs

How do you calculate a percentage manually?

Divide the part by the whole and multiply the result by 100.

Can a percentage be greater than 100?

Yes. A value greater than 100% means the part exceeds the original total.

Where are percentages used?

Percentages are used in statistics, finance, health metrics, academic scores and everyday comparisons.
