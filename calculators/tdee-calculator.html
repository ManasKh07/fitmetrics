What is Total Daily Energy Expenditure?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) represents the total number of calories your body burns each day. It combines your basal metabolic rate with calories burned through physical activity and digestion.

Understanding your TDEE helps determine how many calories you should consume to maintain, lose, or gain weight.

FAQs

Why is TDEE important?

Your TDEE determines how many calories you should eat to maintain your current weight. Eating below it creates a deficit; above it creates a surplus.

How do I lose weight using TDEE?

Consume fewer calories than your TDEE to create a calorie deficit. A deficit of 500 kcal/day typically results in about 0.5 kg of loss per week.

Does exercise increase TDEE?

Yes. Physical activity increases daily energy expenditure, which is why the activity multiplier is factored into the calculation.
