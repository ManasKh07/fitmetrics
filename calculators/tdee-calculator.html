What Is TDEE and How Is It Calculated?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day. It combines your Basal Metabolic Rate (the calories burned at complete rest) with the energy used for physical activity and food digestion. Knowing your TDEE is the most important starting point for any nutrition goal — whether you want to lose fat, build muscle, or maintain weight.

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which research has shown to be the most accurate formula for estimating BMR in the general population. Your BMR is then multiplied by an activity factor to give your TDEE. Use the result with our calorie deficit calculator for weight loss or calorie surplus calculator for muscle gain. FAQs

How accurate is this TDEE estimate?

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is accurate to within 10% for most people. True TDEE can only be measured precisely in a metabolic chamber. Use this as a starting point and adjust based on real-world results over 2–3 weeks.

How often should I recalculate my TDEE?

Recalculate every 4–6 weeks if your weight changes significantly, or if your activity level shifts.

Why is my TDEE different from other calculators?

Different calculators use different equations (Mifflin-St Jeor, Harris-Benedict, Katch-McArdle). Results will vary slightly. Mifflin-St Jeor is the most validated for the general population.
