Understanding Your Macro Results

Macronutrients — protein, carbohydrates and fat — are the three main categories of nutrients that provide energy. Your optimal macro split depends on your goals, activity level and personal preference. This calculator uses your TDEE (calculated from the Mifflin-St Jeor equation) and adjusts for your goal to give you gram targets for each macro.

Learn more in our guide: Understanding Macronutrients. Or calculate your specific protein target in detail.

FAQs

How many calories are in each macro?

Protein and carbohydrates provide 4 kcal per gram. Fat provides 9 kcal per gram. Alcohol provides 7 kcal per gram.

Do macros matter for weight loss?

Yes. While total calories determine weight change, macros affect hunger, muscle retention and energy levels. Higher protein during a deficit preserves muscle mass.

Should I track macros every day?

Tracking for 2–4 weeks builds strong intuition about food composition. After that, many people maintain results without strict daily tracking.
