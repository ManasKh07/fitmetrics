How Much Water Do You Really Need?

Adequate hydration supports every function in the body — from nutrient transport and temperature regulation to cognitive performance and joint lubrication. Most adults need between 2–3.5 litres of fluid daily from all sources. Read our complete guide: Daily Water Intake Guide.

FAQs

Does coffee count toward daily water intake?

Yes. Despite the mild diuretic effect of caffeine, research shows that regular coffee consumption contributes positively to daily fluid balance.

How can I tell if I am well hydrated?

Pale yellow urine (like lemonade) is the simplest indicator of good hydration. Dark yellow or amber urine indicates you should drink more.

Should I drink more water when exercising?

Yes. Add approximately 400–600 ml for every hour of moderate exercise, more in hot weather or for intense training.
