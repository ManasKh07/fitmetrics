Why Exact Age Matters for Health Calculations

Your chronological age directly affects several health calculations. The Mifflin-St Jeor equation used in our TDEE and macro calculators includes age because metabolism slows by approximately 1–2% per decade after age 20. Heart rate zones are calculated from maximum heart rate (220 − age). Understanding your exact age ensures these calculations are as accurate as possible.

FAQs

Does leap year affect age calculation?

Yes. This calculator accounts for leap years and gives you the precise number of years, months and days since your date of birth.

What is the difference between chronological and biological age?

Chronological age is time since birth. Biological age estimates how old your body functions based on health markers, fitness level and lifestyle habits.
