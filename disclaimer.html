Calculator Accuracy and Limitations

All calculators use established formulas including the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (TDEE), Devine formula (ideal weight), Karvonen formula (heart rate zones), and US Army circumference method (body fat), among others. However:

  • All results are estimates, not precise measurements
  • Formula accuracy varies between individuals based on genetics, body composition, ethnicity, age, and health status
  • BMI does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution
  • Calorie estimates carry a typical margin of error of ±10–15%
  • Body fat estimates from circumference measurements carry a typical margin of error of ±3–5%

For clinical-grade assessments, consult a healthcare professional who can perform appropriate diagnostic testing.
