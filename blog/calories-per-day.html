How Many Calories Should You Eat Daily?

Understanding your calorie needs based on age, gender, and how active you are.

Calories are the units of energy that your body uses to perform essential functions such as breathing, digestion, movement, and maintaining body temperature. Every day, your body requires a certain number of calories to function properly. Eating too many leads to weight gain, while consuming too few may cause fatigue, nutrient deficiencies, and loss of muscle mass.

What Are Calories?

A calorie is a measurement of energy. The foods and drinks you consume contain calories that your body converts into fuel — powering everything from your heartbeat and brain activity to walking, exercising, or lifting objects.

Your daily calorie requirement depends on age, gender, weight, height, and activity level.

Average Daily Calorie Needs

GroupAverage Daily Calories
Adult Women1,800 – 2,200
Adult Men2,200 – 2,800
Teenagers2,000 – 3,000
Children1,200 – 2,000

These are averages and may vary depending on lifestyle and metabolism.

Factors That Affect Daily Calorie Needs

1. Age

As people get older, metabolism tends to slow down. Younger individuals usually require more calories because their bodies are still growing and developing.

2. Gender

Men generally require more calories than women because they typically have more muscle mass and higher metabolic rates.

3. Activity Level

Activity LevelDescription
SedentaryLittle or no exercise
Lightly ActiveLight exercise 1–3 days per week
Moderately ActiveModerate exercise 3–5 days per week
Very ActiveHard exercise 6–7 days per week

4. Body Size and Composition

People who are taller or have more muscle mass require more calories because their bodies burn more energy even at rest.

Calories for Weight Loss

To lose weight, you need to consume fewer calories than your body burns — a calorie deficit. A common recommendation is to reduce daily intake by about 500 calories, which can lead to roughly 0.5 kg of weight loss per week. Extremely low-calorie diets can be unhealthy and difficult to maintain.

Calories for Muscle Gain

To gain muscle, you'll need to consume more calories than your body burns — a calorie surplus. A moderate increase of 300–500 extra calories per day combined with strength training can support muscle growth.

How to Estimate Your Calorie Needs

One of the most accurate ways is by calculating your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). Use our free TDEE Calculator to determine this number precisely and adjust your intake based on your goals.

Maintaining a healthy calorie balance doesn't mean counting every calorie obsessively. Focus on balanced meals with protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates — and stay physically active. These habits will naturally keep your calorie intake in a healthy range.
